Ghana Treasury Market Analysis (2021–2025)

Financial Data Analyst

Project Overview

End-to-end data analysis of Bank of Ghana's Treasury Bill (T-Bill) issuances from January 2021 to September 2025. Leveraging Python for data engineering and Power BI for financial modeling to uncover critical trends in interest rates, yield spreads, and market volatility during a historic period of economic adjustment in Ghana.

Key Insights & Findings

1. The Yield Hierarchy

  • Dominant Asset: 364-Day Bill emerged as the most lucrative short-term instrument (Average: 25.1%)
  • Risk Premium: Clear yield curve with 3.7% premium for 1-year commitments over 3-month holdings

2. Market Stress & Spread Analysis

  • Peak Stress (2023): Yield spread reached 4.5 indicating extreme liquidity pressure
  • Stabilization (2025): Spread cooled to 1.8 suggesting macroeconomic normalcy

3. The "December Effect" (Seasonality)

  • Optimal Entry: December offers highest average rates (27.40%)
  • Fiscal Cycle: Yields consistently ramp up in Q4 (Oct–Dec) due to end-of-year government budgetary pressures

Financial Analytics 2024

Ghana Stock Exchange Performance & Risk Assessment

Financial Data Analyst

Executive Summary

A comprehensive evaluation of the Ghana Stock Exchange (GSE) investment landscape using trading data from 2021–2023. This analysis moves beyond simple price tracking to assess true stock quality based on risk-adjusted returns, hidden trading costs (liquidity), and the reliability of price movements.

The analysis revealed a highly fragmented market where Benso Oil Palm Plantation Plc (BOPP) emerged as the most efficient stock (Sharpe Ratio: +3.06), while Trust Bank Limited (The Gambia) offered the best liquidity and most reliable price trends. Conversely, extreme illiquidity rendered several stocks functionally untradeable.

Core Analysis Findings

1. Risk-Adjusted Return (Sharpe Ratio)

Identified stocks that maximize returns while minimizing volatility. Benso Oil Palm Plantation Plc (BOPP) demonstrated superior risk management with a Sharpe Ratio of +3.06.

3. Trading Conviction (Volume-Price Correlation)

Assessed the reliability of price trends by measuring correlation with trading volume.

Conviction Company Correlation Trend Reliability

Data Quality Warning

Analysis revealed severe data quality failures: extreme outliers (-6115% return, +17.48 correlation) due to data sparsity and missing trades. This highlights structural problems for investors relying on automated data feeds for illiquid stocks.

Methodology

  • Cleaned and processed 18,000+ daily trading records
  • Calculated Sharpe Ratios for risk-adjusted performance
  • Computed Average Relative Spreads for liquidity assessment
  • Analyzed Volume-Price Correlations for trend reliability
  • Used Python for statistical analysis and data validation

Strategic Recommendations

For Investment Managers

  • Prioritize stocks with high Sharpe Ratios (BOPP, EGL)
  • Focus on liquid stocks (TBL, MTNGH) for portfolio efficiency
  • Use Volume-Price Correlation to validate investment decisions
  • Exit positions with spreads above 50%

For Regulators

  • Conduct data integrity audit for extreme outliers
  • Address structural illiquidity of problematic stocks
  • Consider different trading tiers for illiquid stocks
  • Implement delisting policies for consistently untradeable stocks
